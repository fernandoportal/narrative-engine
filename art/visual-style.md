# Visual Style Direction

## Visual Thesis

Grounded cinematic realism with high-contrast environments where institutional spaces feel rigid and human spaces feel improvised.

## Palette

- Institutional interiors: desaturated steel, ash, and sodium amber
- Human/private spaces: warmer neutrals with selective saturated accents
- Crisis moments: harsh red warning tones used sparingly

## Composition Principles

- Wider frames for systems and surveillance
- Tighter close-ups during moral decisions
- Negative space to emphasize isolation under pressure

## Recurring Motifs

- reflective surfaces (glass, water, monitor screens)
- threshold imagery (doors, gates, rail switches)
- fragmented typography in signage and interfaces
